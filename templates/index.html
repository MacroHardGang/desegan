Who We Are

We digitalize the process for identifying persons of interest.

The current process for creating a visualization for a person of interest requires hiring a professional artist. This process is not only long and expensive, but human bias will always play a role in these illustrations. Northern GANs automates this process by generating potential images of a suspect based on a text description. This application can be used to identify a variety of targets including criminals, missing children, and fugitives.

How we do it

Our deep learning based approach.

1. Data Collection

Training data was used from two different sources. Images from the Faces in the Wild Dataset were labelled with descriptions generated by the Microsoft Cognitive Services Face API. Additionally, we were able to obtain more labelled data given by the RiVAL AI Research Group from the University of Malta.

2. Face to Text

Additional data to fill the distribution manifolds was generated by Microsoft Cognitive Services Face API. It was theorized that keywords and prose sentences that contain important information would result in similar representations in the latent state space when passed through an LSTM autoencoder.

3. Target Generation

The generation of the data was done using an architecture that was a combination of the Progressive Growing of GANs and Stack GANs, which are currently the two leading technologies in image-generating neural network models.
